# Constraints

The things that bound mentor advice. A great suggestion that
ignores your real constraints isn't a great suggestion.

## Time

Hours per week you can put against the work. What % of those are
maker vs manager hours.

## Team

Solo, contractor-augmented, or team. If team, rough headcount and
shape (eng, content, ops).

## Money

Self-funded, lifestyle-margin, raised, profitable. The relevant
budget axis (cash runway, monthly P&L slack) for thinking about
which mentor suggestions are affordable.

## Energy

Things you have a finite amount of regardless of time — context
switching, deep work blocks, social bandwidth, emotional load.
Mentors don't always weight these correctly.

## Life situation

Geographic, family, health — anything that's a hard constraint
on the optionality mentors might assume you have.

## Hard nos

Tactics or commitments you've already ruled out for non-business
reasons. Saves the council from re-litigating settled questions.
